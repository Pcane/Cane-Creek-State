BARIUM CARBONATE
0.345% of dry batch weight (47g per 30 lb).
N100 mask required for all dry mixing.
Pre-mix with 500g batch material, then with 3-4 lb,
then with full batch.

MIXING SEQUENCE
1. Bentonite slurry the night before
   (0.563 lb dry + 5.63 lb warm water for 30 lb batch)
2. Weigh all dry materials except bentonite
3. Pre-mix barium carbonate (N100 mask)
4. Combine all dry, mix
5. Add bentonite slurry
6. Add remaining water gradually — consistency is the final guide

SECTION 2 — RAISED-LINE TILE PROCEDURE

1. Press tile in mold
2. Dry 3 days
3. Bisque fire
4. Wax back with hot paraffin
5. Full submersion in bowl of water
6. Bulb-apply colored glazes into recesses
7. Dry briefly
8. Quick clear-glaze dip over entire tile
   (vertical, fast continuous arc)
9. Add date and time tiles hit shelves to shelf notes

----------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 3 — FIELD TILE PROCEDURE

1. Press tile in mold (slight center dome)
2. Dry 3 days
3. Bisque fire
4. Wax back with hot paraffin
5. Full submersion in bowl of water
6. Single thin colored-glaze dip in the ordered color
7. Add date and time tiles hit shelves to shelf notes

Fine-tuning final use additives at time of mixing.

             0.25% to 1.00% of dry weight.

SECTION 5 — WORKING BATCHES FROM THE 5-GALLON BASE

The 5-gallon base slurry is mixed once at SG 1.35 / CMC 0.20%.
All working batches are drawn from this base and adjusted
for application.

BULB-SYRINGE RECESS COLORS — 600g batch
Targets: SG 1.28-1.30 | CMC 0.25-0.30%

1. Weigh 600g of base slurry from the 5-gallon stock.
2. Add the G-series colorant per Section 6
   (e.g., 2.424g Cobalt Oxide for G6).
3. SG will sit around 1.35-1.36 after colorant addition.
   Add 15-35g of water to land in 1.28-1.30 range
   (~15-20g for SG 1.30, ~25-35g for SG 1.28).
   Measure with a syringe — small additions.
4. Add small amount of 10% CMC stock to bring CMC up
   from base 0.20% to 0.25-0.30%:
     To reach 0.25% CMC: add ~1.2g of 10% CMC stock
     To reach 0.30% CMC: add ~2.4g of 10% CMC stock
5. Stir thoroughly. Sieve if needed. Bottle.

CLEAR OVERDIP — 120 oz batch (~4,330g)
Targets: SG 1.20-1.25 | CMC 0.10-0.15%

1. Draw ~3,300g of clear base slurry from the 5-gallon stock.
2. Add water gradually while stirring — start with ~1,000g —
   and check SG. Target SG 1.22 (midpoint of range).
3. As SG drops from 1.35 to ~1.22, CMC drops proportionally
   from 0.20% to ~0.14% — this lands inside the target range.
   No additional CMC needed.
4. Total batch should weigh ~4,330g and measure ~3,550 mL
   (~120 oz).
5. Stir thoroughly before each dipping session.
   Settling is more aggressive at this volume.

FIELD TILE COLOR DIP — 120 oz batch (~4,330g)
For the 2-3 selected production colors (green, blue, clear).
Other colors mixed per-order.

Targets: SG 1.20-1.25 | CMC 0.10-0.15%

1. Draw ~3,300g of clear base slurry from the 5-gallon stock.
2. Add the G-series colorant scaled to the working batch —
   multiply the G-series 600g recipe value by 5.5 to get the
   amount for ~3,300g of base:
     Example: G6 Opaque Dark Blue at 1% Cobalt Oxide
              -> 2.424g x 5.5 = 13.3g Cobalt Oxide
     Example: G8 Transparent Green at 3.5% Mason 6254
              -> 8.484g x 5.5 = 46.7g Mason 6254
3. Add water gradually while stirring — start with ~1,000g —
   to bring SG down to ~1.22.
4. CMC will land in target range from base dilution.
   No additional CMC needed.
5. Stir thoroughly before each dipping session.

ALWAYS unplug the kiln and turn off the breaker before
taking any resistance readings. Never take Ohm readings
on a live kiln.

Note: Kiln 2 fires perfectly on the same programs —
problem is Kiln 1 specific, not the program or loading.
Start with element resistance and terminal checks.

Step 1 — Element resistance — all three pin pairs
  How: Unplug kiln. Set multimeter to Ohms. Place probes
  on pins 1&2, then 3&4, then 5&6. Record all three readings.
  Action: Any reading more than 10% above original spec —
  replace that element. L&L recommends replacement at 10%
  increase.

Step 2 — Terminals — loose or corroded
  How: With kiln unplugged, visually inspect all element
  terminal connections. Look for corrosion, discoloration,
  or loose screws.
  Action: Clean corroded terminals. Tighten to spec
  (~20-30 in-lbs). Apply anti-oxidation compound after cleaning.

Step 3 — Voltage under load
  How: With kiln running at full power, measure hot-to-hot
  voltage at the kiln. Compare to reading at the breaker panel.
  Action: More than a few volts drop indicates a wiring
  problem. Call an electrician.

Step 4 — All elements glowing equally
  How: During firing, carefully open lid a crack. All elements
  should glow the same shade of red.
  Action: Any element darker or not glowing has failed.
  Replace it.

Step 5 — Contactors and relays
  How: Listen for contactors clicking during firing. Each
  section should cycle on and off.
  Action: Replace worn or pitted contactors. Inexpensive
  and often overlooked.

Step 6 — Thermocouple accuracy
  How: After ~50 cone 6 firings the thermocouple drifts.
  Fire a cone pack and compare result to controller reading
  at shut-off.
  Action: If controller says F2185 but cone pack shows
  underfiring — replace thermocouple.

CANE CREEK TILE CO. — QUOTE TERMS & CONDITIONS

About Our Tiles
Cane Creek tiles are handmade one at a time in our Alamance County,
NC studio. Natural variation in color, glaze surface, texture, and
dimension is inherent to the handmade ceramic process and is
considered a characteristic of the work, not a defect. We encourage
clients to request sample tiles before placing an order to understand
and appreciate the nature of handmade tile. No two tiles are exactly
alike — this is the nature and beauty of the craft.

Samples
Sample tiles are available upon request. We strongly encourage
clients to review samples before placing an order. Sample tiles are
representative of our standard colorways and finishes but will vary
from production tiles as each firing is unique.

Deposit & Payment
A 50% deposit is required to initiate production. The balance is due
prior to shipment. Orders will not ship until the balance is paid
in full.

Production Samples
For orders of 20 tiles or more Cane Creek Tile Co. will produce
dedicated sample tiles in the ordered colorways and ship them to the
client for approval prior to full production. Sample tiles are shipped
at no charge and count toward the total order quantity. Production
samples will be provided for each colorway that has not been
previously approved via stock sample. For standard colorways
previously approved via stock sample one production sample from the
firing batch will be submitted for approval. Written approval of
production samples is required before full production proceeds.
Approved production samples establish the color and finish standard
for that order. Variation within the approved sample range is
inherent to the handmade process and not considered a defect.

Production Timeline
Full production of approximately 6 to 8 weeks begins upon receipt
of written sample approval. For orders under 20 tiles the production
timeline begins upon receipt of deposit. Production timelines may
vary based on order complexity and studio schedule. We will
communicate any significant delays promptly.

Changes & Cancellations Before Sample Approval
If an order is cancelled before written sample approval is received
a full refund of the deposit will be issued. Changes to confirmed
orders before sample approval are welcome and will be accommodated
at no additional charge. All changes require written confirmation
before work is adjusted.

Changes & Cancellations After Sample Approval
The deposit is non-refundable once written sample approval has been
received and full production has begun. If an order is reduced or
cancelled after production has begun the deposit is retained by
Cane Creek Tile Co. The client will be offered tiles up to the value
of their deposit at the original per-tile price. Any tiles produced
in excess of this amount become studio inventory. All changes after
production has begun require written confirmation before work
is adjusted.

Shipping
Shipping costs for the completed order will be calculated and added
to the final invoice prior to shipment. All orders ship fully insured
for replacement value. Cane Creek Tile Co. is not responsible for
installation costs or project delays resulting from shipping damage.

Shipping Damage
In the event of damage in transit Cane Creek Tile Co. will replace
damaged tiles at no additional charge to the client. Replacement
timelines follow standard production schedules. Please inspect all
shipments upon delivery and note any damage with the carrier before
signing for the shipment. Damage claims must be reported to
Cane Creek Tile Co. within 5 business days of delivery.

Returns
All tiles are made to order and are non-returnable. Manufacturing
defects — defined as structural failures, significant dimensional
irregularities that prevent proper installation, or tiles that
substantially depart from the approved production sample — will be
replaced at no charge. Natural variation inherent to the handmade
process is not considered a manufacturing defect.
